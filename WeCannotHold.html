We Cannot Hold

As I lie on the cold, rugged ground
bones pinch each other
from my spines to my throat, cracking, crack.
In the shades, hungry children and widows
stare at the heart of the tunnel,
stone with an edge of frost.

We cannot hold

Stoves fueled by our own blood and flesh,
in the silent alley of the wretched.
We know only of sorrow but not of pain,
for the privileged has left us in vain.
Approaching sounds of horseshoe stomp on our souls,
each step beats the foam from our blood.
Colder than the ground, the men looked down,
Without lifting an eye, child not but ant.

We cannot hold

Pray for us, plate to us,
the last benevolence in the grasping class.
Lamarque weaves hope in the cold light of day,
feeds our children, touches our souls.
Our fellow men rise to the light, fingers made of wood and steel,
but our dull minds and bodies cannot heal.

We cannot hold
